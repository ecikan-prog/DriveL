# NZTA Research Findings

## Key Work-Time Rules
- 30-minute break after 5.5 hours of WORK TIME (not just driving)
- SPS drivers: 7 hours continuous work time for short fares (<100km)
- 13-hour cumulative work day maximum, then 10-hour rest
- 70-hour cumulative work period, then 24-hour rest
- Full work day: begins after 10-hour rest, max 24 hours

## Logbook Recording Requirements
- Driver name (initials and surname, or first and last name)
- Date the logbook page starts on
- Time of day logbook page starts (am/pm or 24-hour format)
- Registration number of each vehicle driven
- Start and finish distance recorder readings (if vehicle has RUC)
- Start and finish times and LOCATION for all work-time hours
- Start and finish times for all rest breaks and WHERE taken
- Continuous record between 24-hour breaks
- Days off must be recorded on first day back

## Record Keeping
- Completed logbooks must be kept for 12 months
- Driver must give employer copy at end of cumulative work period or within 14 days
- Must show logbook to enforcement officer from last 24-hour rest to present
- Enforcement officer can remove pages

## Electronic Logbook Approval
- Must apply to NZTA (driverandoperator@nzta.govt.nz)
- Must have thorough working knowledge of Work Time and Logbooks 2007
- Must meet required specifications
- 8 currently approved providers (EROAD, Gravy, Logmate, Log Ninja, Sibatec, SmartMove, Teletrac Navman, Trackit, Picobyte/eLogbook)

## SPS Driver Special Rules
- 7 hours continuous work for short fares (<100km in city/town)
- If fare >100km, must take 30-min break after 5.5 hours
- Rest breaks must be uninterrupted by work activities
- Cannot refuel, do paperwork, manage bookings during rest

## Penalties
- Up to $2000 fine per breach for drivers
- Disqualification from driving (min 1 month)
- Up to $25,000 for employers (chain of responsibility)
- Demerit points for logbook offences (10-35 points)
